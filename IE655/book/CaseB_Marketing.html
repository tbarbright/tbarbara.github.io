The Challenge

By focusing on quality design and manufacture in an integrated fashion, and then finding out, and then capturing what customers want, need, or desire, and truly require is essential for every business to survive and prosper.

Furthermore, getting information about new products and/ or services across a customer base is one of the greatest challenges of mankind.

Since information, presented in a clear and meaningful manner is the key to progress, an organization’s success often depends on its ability to communicate.

Our methods and tools, presented in this simulated 3D multimedia eBook program help quality-focused product / process, marketing and sales engineers, product / process and system design engineers and manufacturers, and even after sales support and maintenance engineers, and IT experts winning new customers.

National and international competition forces marketing engineers, designers, manufacturers, demanufacturers, IT engineers, and managers to continuously innovate, collaborate and make their product / process design and manufacturing processes market driven.

This means that marketing and sales departments have to find out what their customer needs, wants, desires and requirements are, and then explain and sell their increasingly more complex products to an increasingly more educated and globally distributed, Internet-ready customer base at a competitive price, and in a short time.

In other words, we have to continuously learn about new customer / user needs, because this drives the innovative process, then create, and simultaneously market new products and / or services.

On the top of all this globalization, and the Internet is changing everything. Companies are designing on an international basis, manufacturing / assembling / maintaining and selling their products directly to well informed global customers over the web 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

Simultaneously the time to market is rapidly shrinking, and the appetite for new, reasonably priced quality products and services is growing. This is why the 'cost of change', 'getting it right the first time', (see again our popular diagram) are essential to simultaneously keep quality high, and cost law... and this can be achieved by means of quality design following CE / PLM principles, methods and tools.

The question in any product design / manufacturing business is who will find out what the customers want, need, and require, and then who will satisfy these requirements first?... because whoever succeeds will get the business!

We can safely state: You! ... if you are using our methods and tools as professionals should do!

Case Based Collaborative Design and Marketing Challenges

Consider, that you work in, and with, a global economy. Both the potential customer as well as the marketing engineer needs to pick the best collaborators, i.e. solution providers. (Yes, we are all in the same boat... even if in some areas we simultaneously collaborate and even compete with each other...)

Following our integrated approach to Case Based Learning as well as Case Based Marketing using our Virtual Product Demo methodology you should be prepared to be able to answer these and similar questions:

  • Does the solutions provider have successful, long term experience with the methods and technologies you require in your industry? Is it a company you can trust?
  • Did you check the references in detail? Did you visit them, seen the systems and talked to the people who actually use it every day? Is the company a leader in the field you are interested in?
  • Does the solutions provider offer a scalable, flexible, lean, reconfigurable, modular solution? (You never know what the future brings... you have to be lean, agile and stay flexible...)
  • Does the solutions provider support open standards? ... and links to intranets/ Internet/ the client - server networking architecture? How difficult is to 'plug-and-play' new devices into such networks? What data can be communicated via such networks and for what purposes? (Could include production control, quality control, remote monitoring, etc.) Did you check this out in detail or just read the machine's user and service manuals...?
  • Does the solutions provider have adequate product information/ on-line support and Virtual Product Demos over the web; preferably in interactive 3D so that you don't have to visit them to see the back of the machine too... Do they / did they demo their work to you? Do they have knowledgeable sales engineers?
  • Are you satisfied with their response times? Is it likely that you'll be satisfied with their delivery times?... correct packing lists, product reliability...quality of technical support?
  • Can the solutions provider offer expertise all along the project from requirements analysis, through system analysis, system design, integration, validation, implementation and maintenance/ service/ upgrade?
  • What if something goes wrong? ... or during the project major changes occur? How would your solutions provider react to that? Could you communicate with their management? Do they anticipate your needs?
  • Does the solutions provider understand management of change? Can they integrate the new machine/ process into your existing design/ manufacturing/ management/ quality assurance culture? Do they make your job easier?
  • Do they help engineers, technicians, operators and maintenance staff to learn about the new system/ process? Do they have on-line. and/or multimedia based learning tools and resources (also called eLearning resources)? (Note, that educated operators, engineers and managers make the difference! The training expense associated with the use of advanced technologies may be holding back small manufacturers from using hi-tech solutions).
  • Do they offer you access to some of the development tools (e.g. software) that you might need to customize your system?
  • Is your solutions provider prepared to help you to develop new, more advanced automation strategies and systems that will create wealth for you? (Do they understand this and support you all the way...?)
  • Can the solutions provider evaluate the economic benefits you'll gain? Are these calculations realistic and sustainable? Did you ask an expert / your team to check the best and worst scenarios? Do they offer objective advice on cost and technology solutions?
  • Does the solution provider treat you as a preferred customer? Do they work as a team and exceed your expectations?
  • An increasing number of businesses learn how the various capabilities of the Internet can be employed to improve their business - to - business (B2B), developer - to - developer (D2D), as well as customer relationships (CR). All signs indicate that it is crucial to go on-line, because competition is rising between companies that are eCommerce ready, and those that are not. Is your solutions provider eCommerce ready?
  • Anything else we, or you have forgotten? Ask your team what do they think of the solution provider(s)... Continuously debate the above issues... brainstorming is an excellent concept if can be performed in a team that truly cares...

Good luck! You'll need it... BUT by deploying the principles, methods, tools and technologies explained in this publication your risks will be significantly reduced.
