    Purpose

    The purpose of this Case Study Chapter is to offer an overview of concurrent engineering design collaboration methods, based on a simple example, and some important tools ised by professionals in industry and academia.

    This case study focuses on an international collaboration between engineers and designers to create a new product. The company HQ is in Toulouse, France. There is an office in London, and as the business is expanding a new hub in New York has just been established.

    Since the HQ is in France, the French Windchill version was taken to the New York office, but the American design engineers are unfamiliar with Catia, so prefer to use ProE instead. This is part of the first large-scale international collaboration between the two branches, and its outcome will direct the future expansion of the company.

    The American engineers speak some French, but by no means fluent. Although English is the preferred international business language, learning not just new software, but in another language has endless challenges.

    The HQ was insistent on using the French version, end of story.

    The tutorials varied in English and French, glossaries were luckily all in English, however they were defining the English terminology, without translations from the French menus and options visible in the Windchill windows. (Defining the terms in English does not help very much if no English words are in the program.)

    Introduction

    The purpose of this project was to create a machine to continuously suspend a product during transport. As part of the design a custom pulley assembly was created, and this case study focuses on the creation, integration, and modification to this assembly using Wildfire and Windchill.

    An assembly created in ProE would be mounted online piece by piece using Windchill, with additional documentation, schematics, spreadsheets, and any other material that would be needed to successfully create a new product.

    For the focus of this study, as well as due to the confidentiality to the company it is not necessary to provide the additional documentation.

    Product lifecycle and design lifecycle were taken into account as well as how to compose the virtual international team to bring the product from the idea to the production stage.

    Drama: Midway through the production stage, the client’s load requirements for the assembly changed and the pulley design as it existed become inadequate.

    Using ProE a new piece was created to add to the existing assembly, then mounted online using Windchill to save the product.

    Along the way various pros and cons of using the software packages together were noted, and possible places for improvement were also documented.

    The main reason that the pulley assembly was chosen for this study was simplicity, its versatility, and importance in the final product design.

    Although relatively simple it leaves many options for making straightforward or complex changes, involved pieces that were prefabricated as well as machined, and was quite simple to demonstrate as a topic.

    ProEngineer

    ProE is one of the world’s most well known CAD packages.

    This powerful 3D modeling engine has variations for CAM simulation, finite element analysis, and animation which made it a perfect choice because all of these features are important tools for developing a new product.

    ProE is meant as the program to create the pieces that will be exchanged, commented on, and modified in Windchill.

    ProE lacks a data exchange option, which is why it is only being utilized for creating and modifying (if necessary) the parts.

    Since both soft wares are PTC productions, there should theoretically be little or no problems using the two together.

    Windchill

    Windchill was developed as a virtual medium for various members of an engineering team to work on a project by exchanging information. Installed on a secure server using Java, design schematics and documentation can be uploaded, modified, copied, or commented on.

    The chief of the project acts online as the administrator to decide upon the level of authority each person has to make changes to existing components. A diagram of the product’s life cycle dictates the direction of the project and what stages need approval (from which teammates) in order to continue.

    For each component the process order can be laid out along side the necessary documentation (structural analysis, parts schematics, and customer order forms). The point behind using a virtual environment is to streamline design time, and allow members to collaborate across continents as if they were in the same building.

    The manner in which Windchill was designed was that each person logs on, and accesses the folder of the project that they are working on, rather than each person having their own virtual desk in Windchill. From this mindset, people think of the project as a group environment, rather than simply a collection of individuals’ contributions.

    With personal, and extraneous documents on the team member’s desktop workstation rather than online, online clutter is minimized.

    Goals of the Pulley Assembly Project

    The major goals of the pulley assembly project were as follows:

    • Successfully design and create the assembly within ProE from AutoCAD schematics.
    • Use Windchill to mount the parts and assembly into the virtual environment.
    • Create a team within Windchill and design the PLC (Product Life Cycle).
    • Document a design error and demonstrate use of Windchill to find a solution.
    • Explain the pros and cons of using the two soft wares together.

    Accessing Windchill

    Since Windchill is an Internet-based medium, a password to the secure server was necessary to access the program.

    This is considered a personal account password. Each member of the team would have a separate user name and password, which would also identify them and log their activity on the project. The first level of access is to get within the program itself.

    From there the user can learn about Windchill’s general functions and become introduced to the icons and navigation system.

    The second level of access is to get to the ‘Explorateur Windchill’ (see Figure 1). This is the bridge between seeing the results of a project, and viewing the components that make it up.

    Within this area one can view the Windchill accounts (armoires) for the company. Whether an armoire is public or private dictates the level of security.

    Creating an Armoire and Dossier

    The organization of Windchill works as a tree. Each armoire has dossiers, and each dossier is a different project. So within each dossier there exist the parts, schematics, and documentation for the project.

    A project chief will have an armoire with their name on it, and the other members of that team open the appropriate dossier within that armoire to upload files. The other members of a team may only have access to the public armoires.

    Some are private and the Project chiefs or administrators are the only ones who can view all projects. So once within the ‘Explorateur’ window as part of a new project, the first step for the chief is to create a new Dossier.

    In this case the ‘Administrator’ Armoire was opened and the new ‘Pulley’ dossier indicated that all documents necessary to the project are located within.

    Building a Team

    Before the project actually begins, the team members must be chosen. Within Windchill the project chief will use this list to decide who is necessary for which steps of the project, and can require their approval in order for certain steps to move forward. At any time, another person can be added to the team, but duplicates of the same job cannot.

    The mindset is that either there is only one person for each function (ex. materials engineer, or marketing), or the people who act collectively as that function agree on decisions before their representative uploads parts onto Windchill, as seen in Figure 1.

    This opens the range of possibilities for a team anywhere from three people, each with a function (design engineer, chief, client) to a team of 50 different functions, each function comprised of several people. The only challenge observed at this point is to create a new type of function.

    Windchill has an extensive list of possible choices for a team, but for a project if a chief would need the approval of the ‘xyz’ person for a design modification then it is not possible to add ‘xyz’ to the list of functions.

    Organizing the flow of information is an important consideration for the project chief. They has the power to control access to each document and article as they see fit. Everyone within the team could be able to make changes and comments on the documents, or access could be restricted to just viewing.

    Product Lifecycle

    Throughout the project, the team will follow the steps laid out in the product lifecycle.

    This can be as simple or complex as necessary to guide the team. For the pulley assembly, the series of steps were laid out assuming that the prototype pieces would be fabricated from scratch by the company’s U.S. machine shop (with production to be outsourced to a factory in Mainland China, others would be purchased from an outside supplier, and certain important steps would need approval from specific team members.

    Figure 2 demonstrates that the Windchill user selects pre-determined operations, lays them out, and can specify who needs to approve what, and when.

    For example, after the development stage, the design would need to be approved by the client, project chief, and the manufacturing engineer.

    But it is not possible to create an option for the life cycle, if the user wanted to create a new sort of prototype stage, it would not be possible to input this into the lifecycle.

    Constructing the Assembly

    There were no complications constructing the parts within proE. Based on design requirements, the parts were sketched out on AutoCAD, and then built using the ProE. Drawing files of the parts were also created and the parts were integrated into an assembly. These were used later on as supplementary material uploaded in Windchill.

    The pulley assembly is composed of four types of components (see CAD mock-up at right):

    • Four Fastening bolts (red): Although custom-made in proE for the assembly, these would be purchased from an outside source and incorporated during product assembly.
    • Carriage Assembly (grey): To be fabricated in house. The process model for this assembly would be included as one of the supporting documents in Windchill.
    • Axle (green): A simple cylinder, cut to dimension, welded to the Carriage Assembly.
    • Pulley Wheel (gold): Cylinder machined to fit specifications laid out in the schematic.

    Creating Documents

    A document can be considered any file of information that is pertinent to the success of the project. This can be an order form for one of the assembly components (the bolt specifications for example), an engineering schematic, a data sheet for a finite element analysis, etc.

    These are made using the ‘create’ button in the ‘Explorateur toolbar’. Each document includes a general description, document number, location to be saved, and file to upload along with the new document. The properties window for each document also includes who created it, when, and who has so far reviewed it.

    Figure 3 is a simple word document with the bolt specification.

    Figure 4 is a .drw file created in proE as a schematic for the pulley wheel. By clicking on the thumbnail of the file the use can also open the files directly, make changes, save it, and then resubmit those modifications for a new iteration (explained later).

    Creating an Article

    Within Windchill, an article is a file that represents a component for the product. By entering the ‘créer article’ window, the team members can create a file for a piece. Each article has a name and number, unique from any other document in the entire network.

    Although at first it may seem strange to keep every piece from every project totally unique, this way a part from another dossier can be used in the current project. Within the window the user can also specify the source of the part (fabricated or bought), quantity, related life cycle, and procedure to obtain it.

    Once completed, by viewing this file the user not just discovers what the piece is, but all the pertinent information to its involvement in the life cycle and where it comes from.

    Créer article: Create Article

    An assembly is a simple variation of an article. By combining several articles together using the same window, one can create an assembly.

    Contained within that file is also the number of each article (the fastening bolts for example) needed for the assembly.

    Making Modifications

    Midway through the production stage, the Pulley Assembly’s load requirements changed. In this case, the client had provided new information to the engineering team regarding the load requirements. A finite element analysis took place on the assembly to check the current design specs under the new load conditions. The site on the Carriage Assembly around the axle was found to be too weak, and deformed under the new load requirements. A design modification is required, but while production has already begun it is always a daunting task to make such a drastic modification so far along the project, as seen in Figure 5.

    In order to relay this information the pertinent team member filled out a ‘Gestionnaire des Modifications’ (see Figure 6) for the problem.

    This is a form recommending a modification to the project. It can be submitted, regarding any component of the product or lifecycle and indicates the nature of the intended modification, urgency, and can be submitted with additional files attached if necessary.

    However before a suggestion can be accepted it must be approved. The form is filed online and the project chief needs to accept it in order for the change to take place. In this case, the project chief reads the form and relays a message to the materials engineer to check the stress analysis results.

    Gestionnaire des Modifications:   Modification Administrator

    At this point the raw materials were already purchased, as were the fastening bolts. To not use some portion would constitute idle resources. The only option was to create a new piece to attach to the existing design.

    Using ProE to create a new piece, the engineers decided to weld a cap to either end of the axle to reinforce the site (see Figure 7).

    Challenges and Solutions

    Once the cap modification has been approved, the new article is incorporated into the assembly file, and the updated schematics are mounted online. When viewing the properties of any document, the window includes the version letter, as well as the iteration number.

    A minor revision of a component will call for an increase in the iteration number, whereas a modification will change the version number.

    All previous versions and iterations can be viewed. In this case, by adding an additional component, the assembly has a new version.

    For any other problems, Windchill can provide access to an online PTC library of references. The user has access to the most up-to-date information as well as being able to email or phone the technical support center.

    Difficulties

    PTC software has difficulty at explaining errors. ProE is excellent at detecting errors when there is a problem, but it is not very good at communicating the problem to the user. Wildfire has similar issues. ‘Error 235%^$0’ does not mean anything to most users.

    Several small tests were used to examine the error messages. For example, when a new article was given the same number as a pre-existing article the error message was completely indecipherable.

    This error can occur because every article in the company’s database is considered as part of the same library. Even after searching the error in the Windchill data bank, no helpful results appeared.

    By keeping an online list of error messages with explanations, users can more easily find out what exactly the problem is, and solve it. This is just one example of how Windchill can frustrate users.

    When challenges like this arose, the best solution was to ask someone else for help. Utilizing the PTC customer support Email was quite effective, a response returning in three days. But it is unrealistic to use tech support for each problem that arises in using the software.

    The version of Windchill used in the project was French. The accompanying manuals, tutorials, and documentation was mostly in French also.

    But the biggest issue is that the glossary of terms was in English. Learning how to use a new program is not too difficult with the proper supplementary documents, but it was impossible to check the function or definition of any of the terminology because none of the English terms were used in this version of Windchill.

    The last critique with using Windchill is making modifications to the terminology used in certain fields. For example when creating a team, lifecycle, or process model the list of choices is quite extensive but leaves no option for creating a new field.

    The non open-source mentality is felt through other pieces of Windchill and although perhaps not everyone should be able to modify these sections, but at least the project chiefs should have the free reign to use terminology they see fit, or be able to add a new choice to the list if necessary.

    Conclusions

    For developing a new product, using proE in conjunction with Windchill creates an overall positive impact on the project. Time and money are the two greatest business concerns when creating a product, and using Windchill to augment the capabilities of proE improves the project in both respects.

    Save Your Project

    According to PTC, 60% of failed projects could have been avoided with better planning. Windchill is termed as a Product Life cycle Management solution and in this respect can save time by keeping information available to the team. By being entirely web-based, any member of the team can remotely access the files at any time they wish, not including server problems.

    This freedom of information can keep up the project pace because the team is not confined to physical limitations like time zones, or locked offices. ProE is still the engine to creating and analyzing the components as an individual task, but designing and modifying these parts is the collaborative portion of PLM.

    There is a clear emphasis on creating a process layout for each component, as well as the life cycle of the product as a whole. When combining this level of planning detail with the free flow of information, Windchill is an excellent tool for improving PLM.

    With an improved PLM approach, cost for errors and manufacturing will also decrease.

    When faced with the challenge midway through, the team would be able to review the problem with the files in Windchill, propose alterations created in proE, and integrate the new parts using both soft wares. What could have normally been a very costly and time consuming modification was spared because all of the information was so easily accessible to the team members.

    Practicality Analysis

    Windchill is an excellent resource for a large-scale international team.

    The virtual space allows ease of access for anyone, regardless of the time of day and allows the receivers of information to examine suggestions and modification at their leisure.

    All resources are documented and recorded in an orderly fashion so by the end of a project, anyone can look back and inspect any part of the project they wish.

    When certain members of the team are not easily reached personally (as an example, consider the 6 hour lead time between Europe and the USA), or with a complicated system, Windchill is a great solution. However in a small business situation or a workshop it is probably more of a hindrance than an aid.

    If the team members all see each other every day then by documenting everything in a virtual office, each team member logging in for every change would just slow down a process (or modification) that would normally be quick and effective. Windchill is also only as effective as the frequency at which the team members access it.

    Although each step of each process is laid out it still requires the human element to keep everything up to date, which can slow down a project with late or inaccurate information.

    Windchill works just as well with Wildfire as probably any other professional CAD package. Although they are both from PTC, there are not any clear advantages of using ProE over Catia or AutoCAD because any modern CAD program can save the part (or assembly) as a STEP file or the schematic as a jpeg imagine. (Standard Template Exchange Protocol; STEP is a standard CAD file format that is used to view and modify CAD designs cross-software and cross-platform.)

    So regardless of whether Catia’s pulley wheel will be rapid prototyped using an SLA system or ProE’s carriage assembly will be fabricated by a CNC machine shop, software choices are only as effective as the CAD skills and preferences of the concurrent engineering team / design engineers using them.
