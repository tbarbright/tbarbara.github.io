    What is Six - Sigma and Why is it Important in CE?

    In the narrow statistical sense, Six - Sigma is a quality objective, developed in the late 1980s by Motorola in the USA, in response of avoiding complex products to fail.

    The purpose of Six - Sigma is to reduce variability, in that it specifies the variability required of a process in terms of the specifications of the product, so that product quality and reliability can meet, and even exceed demanding customer requirements, meaning 3.4 defects, or nonconforming parts/ products, or processes per million opportunities (as per the Motorola, versus the mathematical definition of Six - Sigma...). Motorola's Six - Sigma concept means to reduce the variability of the process parameter values so, that the specification limits are six standard deviations (sigma) from the mean.

    This means, that following this definition mathematically, only about 2 parts per billion will be defective, whereas following the Motorola implementation of Six - Sigma, actually about 3.4 parts per million are accepted to be defective. (Note, that all of these statistical results assume, that the process performance is predictable, since the process behavior is stable.)

    Still a very small number, unless you are fabricating mission critical products, like a pace maker, and yours is one of those 3.4 parts failing out of a million... maybe this example brings home the importance of zero defects!

    In practice, as we state throughout this eBook, Six - Sigma (in integration with other methods) reaches far beyond this narrow definition, in that it includes

    • Leadership,
    • Infrastructure,
    • Statistical, and
    • Process modeling tools, and methods.

    The originators of Six - Sigma have followed the advice of Joseph M. Juran and Peter Senge to make quality part of the entire corporate business plan, from marketing, to design, manufacturing, sales, and even administration and management services.

    General Electric (GE), one of the key originators of the concept, defines Six - Sigma as "a disciplined methodology of defining, measuring, analyzing, improving and controlling the quality in every one of the company's products, processes and transactions, with the ultimate goal of virtually eliminating all defects."

    Although Six - Sigma's tools and methods include many of the statistical tools that were employed in other quality movements, here they're employed in a systematic project-oriented fashion through the well known

    • Define,
    • Measure,
    • Analyze,
    • Improve, and
    • Control. This is often referred to as the DMAIC cycle.

    Note, that Six - Sigma methods, the DMAIC cycle, and our TQM approach to problem solving are in strong harmony, because they represent the same thinking, in terms of a strong TQM methods- base we follow in every Chapter of this eBook, namely:

    • First, analyze the TQM needs and the requirements,
    • Analyze the actual TQM methods presented, find out the core methodologies, the mathematical models, the underlying engineering (and/or other) science foundation.
    • Analyze the TQM technologies involved. (How is science turned into a practical solution/ engineering and/or computing technology? Learn, that science and technology are integrated processes.)
    • Analyze and review the actual processes and the way the process flow is integrated. (Follow an object-oriented process analysis method from concept to product. To understand total quality and integration issues is a major challenge for all of us...)
    • Analyze potential failure modes, and alternative solutions
    • Analyze the Total Quality Management aspects of each process, as well as the end product/ service.
    • Analyze the benefits and the disadvantages of each process/ solution.
    • Design alternative TQM methods, processes based on what you have experienced/ seen, and learned.
    • Design an integrated TQM system, based on what you have analyzed.
    • Work in a multi-disciplinary TQM team and exchange ideas
    • Understand the boundaries of TQM, as well as the tremendous potential of new ideas and developments by working on the cases presented. (Realize that in order to survive and win, you must add quality value).

    Six-sigma was developed (originally by Motorola) to pursue the same target as the Baldrige criteria: completely satisfying customer needs profitably.

    One major difference seems to be that Baldrige criteria show just the shell of targets within those criteria whereas Six Sigma also describes a proven box of powerful tools and strict guidelines for applying them.

    How to Perform the Six - Sigma Process? A Concurrent Engineering Design Focus with Industrial Examples

    In order to keep a manufacturing and/or assembly system, and a factory, that hosts advanced systems at its peak efficiency, to be able to react to customer orders on time, and to be able to take decisions at the process levels, at the time required, based on dynamically changing market conditions, we need a real-time performance evaluation and control model with integrated sensory feedback processing for the machine-designated decisions, and real-time sensor driven, computed visualization, preferably in 3D, for human operators and managers. In terms of product, process and service system design, the challenges are very similar, therefore six-sigma can be applied in CE and design processes too.

    In real-time controlled digital factories performance management is based on real-time data, therefore continuous monitoring and Six-Sigma analysis is essential.

    Resources are allocated based on dynamic customer needs, and humans and machines are empowered to change action plans as required.

    Coupled with real-time requirements analysis, to satisfy  dynamically changing customer requirements, with real-time process analysis, to reduce variation and monitor trends, and with real-time process risk analysis, to prevent and reduce failure, such systems become very powerful, therefore attractive to investors.

    Real-time systems of this nature offer process-embedded, real-time activated controls, with synchronization and conflict resolution, integrating behavior that share resources and missions. (Example lean cell layout.)

    Furthermore in such distributed systems each process contains the link to the logic and the sensor(s) / machinery, along with their interfaces to the embedded lower level services upon which they rely.

    Such systems are very similar to nature, in which internal, and external customer needs, and market realities dictate conditions for action and ultimate survival.

    The solution in such complex real-time systems is Six -Sigma. The Six - Sigma process, as performed by TQM driven corporations is typically as follows:

    1. Define the goals of the improvement activity. This can be very small, like a few variables in an assembly process, or welding process, or others, as well as major, like improve every process in a department; nevertheless it is advisable to start small. Note, that your process model, the requirements and the risk analysis results will clearly identify where to start, because risky processes, that customers need, or want should be at the top of your list of candidates for improvement).

    2. Analyze and measure the process output (i.e. the selected parameter value(s)) of the process under study.

    3. Analyze and measure the process inputs, controls and resources (think of the CIMpgr process model notations) for criticality. (Criticality here means long setup times, long wait and hold times, non JIT-like, lean production, bottlenecks, piling up inventories, in balances in production lines between workstations, or cells, jamming and failing parts, and others.) The aim is obviously to reduce criticality, and then avoid it entirely, if possible.

    4. Improve the process by, based on your analysis and measurements, modifying the necessary inputs, controls and resources (think of the CIMpgr process model notations).

    5. Use statistical methods to validate the improvement(s), or change.

    6. Continuously monitor, and control the process (preferably using a closed-loop, versus open-loop architecture, in which reliable sensors feed back real-time monitored data directly from the process into PLCs, and a network of computers.)

    To summarize, the above methodology, coupled with real-time requirements analysis, to satisfy  dynamically changing customer requirements, with real-time process analysis, to reduce variation and monitor trends, and with real-time process risk analysis, to prevent and reduce failure, such systems become very powerful, therefore attractive to investors.

    Real-time systems of this nature offer process-embedded, real-time activated controls, with synchronization and conflict resolution, integrating behavior that share resources and missions.

    Furthermore in such distributed systems each process contains the link to the logic and the sensor(s) / machinery, along with their interfaces to the embedded lower level services upon which they rely.

    Such systems are very similar to nature, in which internal, and external customer needs, and market realities dictate conditions for action and ultimate, sustainable survival.

    If you are an engineering/ science student make sure you understand and document the following:

    • What are the analytical / mathematical/ material science aspects of the illustrated Six - Sigma TQM methods, challenges and solutions? Upon what scientific principles do they operate?
    • What are the major Six - Sigma TQM processes that make the machines / services operate as illustrated? (Create an object-oriented process model showing how objects / modules interact with each other).
    • How are these processes integrated and controlled from a quality perspective? What are the process parameters and the limitations? How can you measure the process parameters, and then quality control them?
    • What are the major Six - Sigma CE/TQM application areas of the machines/ processes discussed in this case?
    • How do you see the future of quality designs, from a concurrent product/ process, or in other words, from a modular, reusable-component design point of view?
    • As a TQM manager in charge, how would you assure, that quality is designed into the product / process / service?

    If you are a computer science/ computer engineering student focus on the quality aspects of software of the machines and/ or processes / services illustrated.

    • On the basis of object oriented process models try to develop a software architecture that could make the illustrated Six - Sigma processes work without any crashes, and software bugs.
    • Try to create the object/ class diagram of a digital communication-enabled product, linked to a digital service model, controlling and operating devices (on-site, or remotely) following Six - Sigma CE/TQM principles.
    • Try to come up with TQM product / service operation control algorithms and document them in pseudo-code, or by using a programming language, such as C++, or Java.
    • Create user quality interface designs, networking (intranet/ Internet) and test procedures for your software project. Using our spreadsheet templates, show how customer satisfaction quality can be assured.
    • How could you turn networked machines / processes / services into web-pages and work with them locally and remotely for the purpose of monitoring their quality behavior? What are the benefits of having machines / processes / services networked in a CE/TQM client-server architecture? Think about this idea and model different client-server solutions and applications (e.g. remote operation control, remote maintenance, remote quality control, and others).
    • What operating system would you chose for satisfying Six - Sigma CE/TQM requirements, considering the fact, that robustness, and fail-safe operation is essential? (No time to reboot when running at 65 miles per hour...). Please explain why, considering quality, development, cost, run-time, maintenance, and other issues.
    • How do you see the future of quality software from a computer science and software design point of view? (How would you increase the quality of software? How would you be able to justify such development costs? (In order to help you with this task, please look up the Software Engineering Code of Ethics and Professional Practice, within this eBook in .html, or in MS-Word).

    • Marketed/ advertised nationally as well as internationally over the Internet following CE/TQM / PLM principles, Virtual Product Demos and other ideas, and by other means (e.g. quality trade shows and special events),
    • Cost-justified to a variety of design and user communities,

    • Furthermore, create a spreadsheet that helps potential customers to cost justify the purchase, lease, and other options of the above CE/TQM solutions and services with all necessary 'what-ifs' that individual salesmen can customize and present professionally at meetings, or over the web.
    • Based on our Case Based Marketing methods and ideas, try to come up with a business plan, and an engineering management project plan that can be customized for each customer interested in purchasing, leasing, etc. the illustrated machines / processes / services.
    • How would you offer your services as a Six - Sigma CE/TQM sales consultant? Create a business plan and a marketing plan for this idea.
    • How would you apply CE/TQM principles for security / safety applications? On what basis would you sell such application opportunities? Develop a marketing and a business plan.
    • How would you approach designers, and insurance companies with TQM options in modern automobiles, and other products and processes?
    • How would you introduce, manage and sell Six - Sigma CE/TQM methods and principles in a company, that at present produces poor quality products, processes, services? How would you start? Create a plan.
    • Based on the current culture of the organization, create a CE/TQM plan and a presentation that evaluates and justifies the above illustrated processes and the way they could be integrated into an integrated quality product/ process design strategy for a small, a medium and a large organization/ company, that specializes retrofitting existing products / services with various levels of CE/TQM solutions.

    • How could you improve the dimensions of quality of a product, or process (including service)?
    • How do you see the future of Six - Sigma CE/TQM from a management and marketing point of view? Would this be a cost effective solution? How about reliability, and quality of service? Present a strategy to the board.
    • Gap analysis: Conduct a marketing study that could lead to the identification of quality gaps in a product / service, or process. What is the need that is not fulfilled at present? Discuss these ideas with your engineering and computing science team members, as well as selected, potential customers. (Try to set up your own business and implement your ideas!)
