Press Release by the Ford Motor Company (2005):

FLEXIBLE MANUFACTURING COMES TO FORD MOTOR COMPANY'S MICHIGAN TRUCK PLANT BODY SHOP


• Ford Motor Company is investing $300 million in a 240,000-square-foot body shop expansion at the Michigan Truck Plant.

• The expansion will bring flexible tooling to the body shop, allowing Ford to react more quickly to changing customer demands at a significant cost savings.

• The body shop addition will expand the production capabilities of the plant – as part of the company's commitment to producing more new products, faster. Details on any future products there will be announced at a later date.

DEARBORN, Mich., Dec. 15 – Ford Motor Company announced today a $300 million investment in a 240,000-square-foot, flexible body shop expansion at the Michigan Truck Plant in Wayne, Mich.

"This has been a tough, but gratifying year in manufacturing for Ford. We've transformed our plants to bring more products, faster to our customers," said Matt DeMars, Ford vice president, Vehicle Operations. "Our goal is to build stability and standardization into our North American assembly and stamping plants, which is what flexible and lean manufacturing is all about."

Ford has launched a record number of vehicles this year – and is on track to meet its commitment of introducing flexible manufacturing into 75% of its body shops, trim and final assembly operations by the end of the decade. Flexibility in the body shop is the most important component of flexible manufacturing because of the complexity and cost of the operation. Ford's new flexible body shop system costs less than both traditional body shops and other flexible systems.

Ford kicked off an aggressive launch year with production of the F-150 at the Dearborn Truck Plant; the Ford GT at the Wixom Assembly Plant; the Escape Hybrid at the Kansas City Assembly Plant; the Ford Five Hundred, Freestyle and Mercury Montego in Chicago; the Mercury Mariner at the Ohio Assembly Plant; and the Ford Mustang at AutoAlliance International.

DeMars attributes their launch successes to the Company's focus on quality, processes – and the people who build the vehicles.

"We are committed to getting our launches right. We know that a strong launch is critical to a vehicle's success," he said. "The Ford Production System, our Launch Quality Operating Systems and our Safety Leadership Initiative are important tools in our manufacturing system."

The Michigan Truck Plant produces the Ford Expedition and Lincoln Navigator. It employs more than 3,000 hourly employees. The body shop addition will expand the production capabilities of the plant. Details on future product will be announced at a later date.

"This is an extremely exciting time for this plant and for the employees here," said Gary Johnson, plant manager. "It reinforces to everyone our future, the long-term viability of the Michigan Truck Plant and our workforce. The new body shop expansion and flexible tooling give us a strong future with the capability of producing exciting new products."


Ford Motor Company, headquartered in Dearborn, Mich., is one of the world's largest automakers, with approximately 318,000 employees in 200 markets on six continents. Its automotive brands include Aston Martin, Ford, Jaguar, Land Rover, Lincoln, Mazda, Mercury and Volvo. Its automotive-related services include Ford Credit, Quality Care and Hertz. Ford Motor Company observed its 100th anniversary on June 16, 2003.
