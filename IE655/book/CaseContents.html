Welcome! The Focus of this interactive 3D eBook is: Concurrent, or simultaneous product, process and service system design engineering within a lean six-sigma quality and sustainable green PLM framework.

It offers a systematic overview and in-depth discussions of world class integrated engineering design principles, with a strong quality focus, in the PLM (Product Lifecycle Management), digital design and manufacturing domain. It promotes the fact, that there is no good design without understanding the full, preferably green product lifecycle, including manufacturing, assembly, maintenance, demanufacturing, and even reuse / recycling. The material represented in this 3D eBook helps the reader to create better, greener, and lower cost products, processes and service systems.

It follows a browser readable, interactive 3D multimedia case-based, analytical, quantitative, open-source computational eBook approach. It includes text, images, active code to calculate with, digital videos, animation, 3D objects, 3D panoramas, 3D virtual facility tours, and active-code software tools for the users to master the subject, and to calculate with their own data.

Concurrent / Simultaneous Engineering with Sustainable PLM (Product Lifecycle Management) is an extremely important subject in our Industrial Internet of Things century. The convergence of machines and intelligent data generated by humans as well as machines in an integrated system is known as the Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT), or in other words, the Internet of Everything (IoE). IIoT encompasses every industrial and commercial device that uses the Internet Protocol (IP), generating data on the same network. We are heading into an increasingly real-time, sensory-feedback-controlled, instrumented and networked world, in which machine and human intelligence is widely distributed at every level of an enterprise, including its global supply chains.

Machines in an IIoT system can be literally anything. Examples include: sensors on cars talking to IIoT data centers (in the cloud) that can communicate to mobile phones to optimize routes, or energy management aspects of the automobile, or collision avoidance, or a robotic machining cell, to blood pressure monitors, to smart machines that package medication at zero-defect levels in a pharmaceutical company. With the IIoT we are also facing the communication and data analysis / control / data security challenges of billions of things communicating with each other. The good news though, is that the Industrial Internet makes industries become leaner, more effective and efficient, higher quality and more profitable. This is because these new ecosystems of connected smart machines have the potential to increase efficiency, minimize waste, and make people operating them think and act smarter. The Industrial Internet is changing the way we work and we have to prepare for this by designing and implementing these systems, as well as directing and enjoying the benefits of this major transition.

The IIoT is an estimated 15 trillion dollar engineering business opportunity and Concurrent / Simultaneous Engineering with Sustainable green PLM and lean six-sigma quality management are essential parts of it.

In product and process design, sustainable green means waste reduction, and optimization, therefore advocates lean six-sigma concepts and methods. In terms of green sustainability we consider an eco-friendly state, or process that can be maintained over time, for a very long time. Also, the ability of an ecosystem to maintain ecological design and manufacturing processes and functions, biological diversity, and productivity over time.

Furthermore, sustainable green design engineering encompasses the concept of meeting present needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their needs.

In other words, the characteristic of green, sustainable advanced quality design, manufacturing, service, etc. is being able to coexist with another system indefinitely, without either system being damaged.

The sustainable green concept also emphasizes, that the creation of wealth within the community considers the wellbeing of both the human as well as natural environments, and is focused on the more complex processes of development rather than on simple growth or accumulation only.

Advanced, sustainable green design and manufacturing / process activities are used in many areas, including product and process design, control, fabrication, quality assurance, quality control, test, assembly, disassembly, packaging, remanufacturing / recycling, logistics, and others.

In terms of collaborative global product / process development, understanding the customers' culture, the environment, cost issues, quality needs, national an dinternational standards / regulations, and other aspects are critical. Our new simulated 360 degree panoramas embedded into a map, with added images and videos help to solve these, and many other challenges. Enjoy!

On the product design marketing side, you will see and experience how our novel 3D interactive Virtual Product Demo (VPD) method works for understanding complex challenges, and systems, as well as for eMarketing in the CE context.

In this eBook we follow innovative, research-focused integrated product, process, and system design thinking principles.

Integrated design thinking draws upon logic, imagination, intuition, and systemic reasoning, to explore possibilities of what could be, and to create desired outcomes that benefit the end user (the customer). A design mindset is not problem-focused, it is solution focused, and action oriented. It involves both analysis and imagination.

“Design is the action of bringing something new and desired into existence—a proactive stance that resolves or dissolves problematic situations by design. It is a compound of routine, adaptive and design expertise brought to bear on complex dynamic situations.” —Harold Nelson

What are the distinguishing characteristics of Design Thinking?
• Finding simplicity in complexity
• Beauty as well as functionality
• Improving quality of experience
• Creating elegant solutions
• Serving the needs of people

Design thinking represents a strategy for innovation. When design principles are applied to strategy and innovation the success rate for innovation dramatically improves. Design thinking is at the core of effective strategy development and organizational change. Design can be applied to products, services, processes, physical locations… anything that needs to be optimized for human interaction. You can design the way you lead, manage, create and innovate..
