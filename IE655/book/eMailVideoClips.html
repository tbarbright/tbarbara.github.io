The Novel Hi-tech Marketing Idea

In order to help you to excite your potential customers about the case we are presenting in this program we have included a separate folder called EmailClips that has some short video clips and perhaps other 'goodies' that you can direct e-mail to your customers, team members and others.

The Solution

If you want to send any of the specially rendered, relatively small, web-enabled video clips to a customer, or team member send it as a standard attached file. (The same way as you attach an MS-Word or any other file to your e-mail.)

All you have to do is to open the 'EmailClips' folder (directory) in this program from your e-mail message window, select the appropriate clip and then 'Attach' it to your e-mail, by clicking on 'Attach'.

Depending on the connection speed your partner is using you can choose between different file sizes. If your partner has a relatively slow but widely used 56 KFlex modem, choose files that are under 300 - 500 kbytes. (Depending on traffic such files should download under 40 - 60 seconds).

If your partner has a faster ISDN, or DSL, or cable modem web-link you can e-mail larger files too without any complaints... (on average they'll take a second or so per 300 Kbytes).

Obviously you can review these special web quality files just by clicking on any of the following larger files and better quality rendering for FAST web-connection (e.g. ISDN, DSL, cable modem, T1 lines, and others):

MiniCooperPano2.mov (1.8 Mbyte video clip)

Rav4600.mov (2 Mbyte video clip)

ToyotaAvensysInsPano.mov (1.5 Mbyte video clip)

The results of e-mailing a video-clip, or optionally a 3DVR or 360 panoramic clip should be very positive, as long as you don't bug down anybody's email system with some huge files.

'Huge' is a relative term in the Internet age..., meaning huge for them, NOT necessarily for you...keep this basic rule in mind.

If you wish to experiment before creating a major mail-shot try to send a video-clip to yourself, and/or a colleague, or friend and 'test the field'... Have fun! They'll like it because everybody gains.

Note, that following the above outlined technique you can directly e-mail any of the media files of this program to your partner(s). (For your information the .mov files are either video clips (some are huge for the web, be warned !), or 3DVR object movies, or 360 degree, often animated panoramas). Since this is an open source program, all you have to do is click on them to review them.

PLEASE OBEY THE COPYRIGHT LAWS! Please make sure that these files are not distributed and sold to any third parties, since that would be against the copyright law. Obviously you should encourage your partner(s) to e-mail these files further, creating a wider and wider marketing opportunity for you!

IMPORTANT!

In your e-mail please make sure that you mention that the clip needs a digital video player, such as Apple's QuickTime that can be freely downloaded from the http://www.apple.com/quicktime/download website.

If you include the above line exactly by copy-pasting it into your e-mail message the direct link to the apple.com site will be automatically enabled when the e-mail arrives to your partner(s), so that they can download the driver and view the clip quickly.

Note, that other media players will most likely be able to play these video-clips too, with the exception of the optional 3DVR and 360 panoramic objects, nevertheless Apple's QuickTime solution will offer without any doubt the best quality! (Try it if you don't believe us...at the time of writing over 150 million plus users (!) have downloaded this excellent piece of multi-platform, streamed multimedia code).
