Why Do We Have to Establish and Follow Quality Standards for Design Activities, Such as CE?

Quality standards in any design activity do:

  • Assure conformity,
  • Create traceability,
  • Offer discipline,
  • Keep known and allowed / agreed levels of variance,
  • Offer interchangeability,
  • Reduce cost,
  • Offer excellent business opportunities (to beat competitors),
  • Improve and sustain customer satisfaction.

Quality standards in CE are essential. They are established and maintained by quality control, quality management, and quality engineering.

As we have stated earlier, quality control means establishing and maintaining specified quality standards of products and processes (including service).

Quality engineering is the establishment and execution of tests to measure product / process quality and adherence to acceptance criteria specified in the standards.

Let us discuss quality management with a CE focus in more detail.

The Eight Quality Management Principles on Which the Quality Management System Standards of the Revised ISO 9001:2008 Series are Based

Based on the latest ISO interpretation, this section introduces the eight quality management principles upon which the quality management system standards of the revised ISO 9001:2008 series are based.

The principles are derived from the collective experience and knowledge of the international academic, industrial, management, and research experts who participate in the ISO Technical Committee ISO/TC 176, entitled: Quality Management and Quality Assurance, which is responsible for developing and maintaining the ISO 9001 standards for all of us.

These principles can be used by senior management as a framework to guide their organizations towards improved quality performance, as well as by all of us.

Our discussion follows the guidelines, the spirit and the terminology, that the eight quality management principles define in the ISO 9001:2008 documents, entitled Quality Management Systems Fundamentals and Vocabulary, and in ISO 9004:2000, Quality Management Systems Guidelines for Performance Improvements.

Note, that we list the eight basic principles below, and then in this, and in other Chapters of this eBook, we'll discuss each principle in more detail, with more examples, related methods, and often with software tools.

.

ISO TQM Standard Principle 1: Customer Focus: Organizations depend on their customers, and therefore should understand their current and future needs, as well as should meet customer requirements, and strive to exceed customer expectations. (Note: the customer here is not just the end-user, but your design team members along th eentire supply chain too).

Key Drivers and Achievable Gains Include the Following:

  • Increased revenue and market share can be obtained through flexible and fast responses to market opportunities. (Obviously the key is to figure out ahead of the competition what the market wants, needs, or desires... ref.: or CORA method, see later in this eBook).
  • Increased effectiveness in the use of the organization's resources to enhance customer satisfaction.
  • Improved customer loyalty leading to repeat business.
  • Customer needs and expectations are understood better than before.
  • Organizational objectives become better linked to customer needs and expectations.
  • Customer needs and expectations are better communicated throughout the organization.
  • Customer satisfaction, as well as the actions taken on the results become measurable.
  • Customer relationships become systematically managed at all levels of the organization.
  • A balanced approach is ensured between satisfying customers and other interested parties (such as owners, employees, suppliers, financiers, local communities and the society as a whole).

.

ISO TQM Standard Principle 2: Leadership: Leaders establish unity of purpose and direction of the organization. They should create and maintain the internal environment in which people can become fully involved in achieving the organization's objectives.

Key Drivers and Achievable Gains Include the Following:

Under a strong, clear and fair CE/TQM leadership....

  • People will see the big picture of integrating customer requirements, with process capabilities, service support requirements, cost, quality and other issues... they will understand, and be motivated towards the organization's goals and objectives (... assuming, that the goals and principles are communicated clearly, and that the resources are made available to implement the desired and realistic goals...).
  • Activities are evaluated, aligned and implemented in a unified way.
  • Miscommunication between levels of the organization will be minimized.
  • The needs of all interested parties can be considered, including customers, owners, employees, suppliers, financiers, local communities, and the society as a whole.
  • A clear vision of the organization's future can be established.
  • Challenging goals and targets can be set.
  • Shared values can be created and sustained, and fairness and ethical models can be created at all levels of the organization.
  • Trust can be established, and fear can be eliminated.
  • People can be provided with the required resources, training and freedom to act at a clearly established level of responsibility and accountability.
  • Employees' contributions will be inspired, encouraged and recognized.

.

ISO TQM Standard Principle 3: Involvement of People: People (or in other words, 'knowledge workers') at all levels are the essence of an organization and their full involvement enables their abilities to be used for the organization's benefit. CE has a strong cultural foundation, therefore able people and their input is essential.

Key Drivers and Achievable Gains for CE/PLM Include the Following:

  • Successful organizations need motivated, committed and involved people who understand the multi-faceted nature of CE and PLM with a Quality focus.
  • Innovation and creativity is expanding the organization's objectives.
  • Employees are accountable for their own performances.
  • Employees should be eager to participate in and contribute to continual improvement.
  • Employees should understand the importance of their contribution and their roles in the organization.
  • Employees should be able to identify the constraints of their performances.
  • Employees should accept the ownership of problems and it should be their responsibility to solve them.
  • Employees should evaluate their performances against their personal goals and objectives.
  • Employees should be actively seeking opportunities to enhance their competence, knowledge and experience levels.
  • Employees should freely share knowledge and experience (within confined circles, as business confidentiality allows).
  • Employees should openly discuss problems and issues with each other.

.

ISO TQM Standard Principle 4: Process Approach: A desired result is achieved more efficiently when activities and related resources are managed as a process. (Note, that we offer a formal CE process description in Chapter 5 in this eBook).

Key Drivers and Achievable Gains for CE/PLM Include the Following:

  • Lower costs and shorter cycle times can be achieved, through effective and controlled use of resources.
  • Improved, consistent and predictable results can be offered to both internal, as well as external customers.
  • Focused and prioritized improvement opportunities become reality, since each process step is identified and controlled (in a similar fashion to an object, with it's identifier, attributes, and behavior, when following object-oriented system modeling principles; example).
  • Activities are defined systematically, therefore the desired result can be obtained realistically.
  • Clear responsibility and accountability can be established for managing key activities in literally any organization.
  • The capability of key activities can be analyzed and measured
  • The interfaces and communication paths of key activities within, and between the functions (or in other words classes of objects) of the organization can be identified.
  • By focusing on factors such as resources, methods, and materials key activities of the organization can be improved.
  • By evaluating process failure risks, consequences and impacts of activities on (internal, as well as external) customers, suppliers and other interested parties becomes possible, and measurable.

.

ISO TQM Principle 5: Systems Approach to Management: Identifying, understanding and managing interrelated processes as a system contributes to the organization's effectiveness and efficiency in achieving its objectives.

Key Drivers and Achievable Gains for CE/PLM Include the Following:

  • Processes that will achieve the desired results will become better integrated and aligned.
  • Management and process owners will have the ability to focus their efforts on the key processes.
  • Since the consistency, effectiveness and the efficiency of the organization will grow, the confidence of interested parties in the organization will grow too.
  • System structuring and tuning will become possible to achieve the organization's objectives in the most effective and efficient way.
  • Understanding the interdependencies between the processes of the system will yield good results.
  • Structured (and object/ component-oriented) modeling approaches that harmonize and integrate processes will become reality.
  • The resistance created by cross-functional barriers will be reduced, providing a better understanding of the roles and responsibilities necessary for achieving common objectives .
  • Organizational capabilities, and the establishment of resource constraints prior to action will be better understood by all involved (and mostly by all those who have created the models).
  • Targeting and defining how specific activities within a system should operate become reality.
  • Continually improving the system through measurement and evaluation becomes possible, due to the analytical and quantifiable approach of the process models.

.

ISO TQM Principle 6: Continual Improvement: The continual improvement of the organization's overall performance should be a permanent objective of the organization.

Key Drivers and Achievable Gains for CE/PLM Include the Following:

  • Performance advantage through improved organizational capabilities.
  • Alignment of improvement activities at all levels to an organization's strategic intent.
  • Flexibility to react quickly to opportunities.
  • A consistent organization-wide approach to the continual improvement of the organization's performance.
  • Employees should be offered training in the methods and tools of continual improvement.
  • Continual improvement of products, processes and systems should be an objective for every individual in the organization.
  • Employees and management should establish goals to guide, and measure to track continual improvement.
  • Management should recognize and acknowledge improvements.

..

ISO TQM Principle 7: Factual Approach to Decision Making: Effective decisions are based on the analysis of accurate data and information. (Information is data about data). Test the correctness of both your data, as well as analytical models from many angles, and many reliable sources.

Key Drivers and Achievable Gains for CE/PLM Include the Following:

  • Informed decisions (can only be made when the data is reliable and accurate). Design is a cultural issue too. Understand your customers. Live with them, talk to them, and see what they truly need.
  • An increased ability to demonstrate the effectiveness of past decisions through reference to factual records.
  • A factual approach increases the ability to review, challenge and change opinions and decisions.
  • Management must ensure, that data and information is sufficiently accurate and reliable.
  • Data should be made accessible to those who need it (within a security framework).
  • Data and information should be analyzed, based on validated, robust methods.
  • Decisions and actions should be based on factual data and information analysis, balanced with experience and intuition.
  • Use CE , CAD/CAM analysis and simulation tools to understand your systems better.

.

ISO TQM Principle 8: Mutually Beneficial Supplier Relationships: Even if an organization and its suppliers are interdependent, a mutually beneficial relationship enhances their ability to create value. PLM, or Product Lifecycle Management systems in CE aim to develop mutually beneficial relationships between designers and suppliers. The rsult is lower cost, and higher quality.

Key Drivers and Achievable Gains Include the Following:

  • There is increased ability to create value for both parties.
  • The flexibility and speed of joint responses to changing market or customer needs and expectations becomes a possibility.
  • Optimization of costs and resources is possible.
  • Relationships can be established, that balance short-term gains with long-term considerations.
  • Pooling of expertise and resources with partners can be beneficial.
  • Identifying and selecting key suppliers is possible.
  • Clear and open communication is required.
  • Sharing information and future plans becomes a business reality.
  • Establishing joint development and improvement activities becomes a possibility.
  • Inspiring, encouraging and recognizing improvements and achievements by suppliers is beneficial to all parties.
  • Recognizing opportunities for improvements is beneficial to all parties.

An Overview of ISO (the International Organization for Standardization)... the Way the ISO Works...

According to the official ISO statements, 'ISO’s role has evolved over the first fifty years of its existence and will no doubt continue to do so in the future. ISO and US standardization bodies are assential for design compatibility, safety, quality, ergonomics, ecology, environmental and many other aspects.

Through the decisions of the Council, ISO responds to its members’ wishes; they in turn respond to the market’s, and thus when new requirements come to be felt for more streamlined procedures to develop normative documents specifically for committees for whom speed is all-important, ISO has responded by developing new products (meaning standards) to cater for these needs.

When ISO was created in 1947, its objective was essentially to provide recommendations to its members aimed at harmonizing national standards, and for the first 25 years of its existence, the results of ISO’s technical work were published as ISO Recommendations.

It was in the early 1970s that ISO began to publish International Standards... (very, very late, agreed?)

By the early 1980s, ISO was starting to be accepted and ISO standards had their own validity in the marketplace.

Towards the latter part of the decade we saw the first signs of what has come to be known as the globalization of markets.

Not only were ISO standards starting to establish their own validity in the marketplace, but in many instances ISO was being asked to prepare the first standards on particular topics, and was consequently moving away from its original mission of harmonizing national standards...'

Since global markets need international standards, the future of ISO, as well as other national (meaning U.S. and other national) as well as international standardization efforts are assured.

An Overview of U.S. Quality Standards (QS-9000)

Whilst US companies accept and subscribe to ISO total quality standards, QS-9000 is a US quality standard, that contains specific requirements and recommendations for the development of a quality system, that meets the expectations of giants, such as Daimler-Chrysler, the Ford Motor Company, General Motors, various truck manufacturers, and others, that supply to these giants.

QS-9000 is NOT a product standard. It does not contain any requirements with which a product, or service can comply. (It is based on ISO 9001).

As a result, one cannot inspect a product against QS-9000. The inspector, however can question whether a particular product has a certain record, an identity, inspection status, or any non conformances recorded, etc.

The main issue about QS-9000 is that products cannot meet this standard, organizations can!

QS-9000 represents the requirements and recommendations that apply to the organizations that supply the product, or service, and hence affect the manner in which the products and services are designed, manufactured, installed, and delivered.

They are standards, that apply to the management of the organization and only the management can and should decide how it will respond to these requirements and recommendations.

QS-9000 is fundamentally a suite of documents, based on ISO 9001, that includes the following:

  • Quality System Assessment Guide (QSA)
  • Advanced Product Quality Planning and Control Plan Reference Manual
  • Potential Failure Mode and Effects Reference Manual (FMEA)
  • Production Part Approval Process Manual (PPAP)
  • Measurement System Analysis Reference Manual
  • Fundamental Statistical Process Control Reference Manual

The above listed topics are referenced within QS -9000 for different industries, such as automotive, semiconductors, and others. (Note, that these are significant, multiple-hundred page documents, that include suppliers too).

The nice feature of the QS-9000 quality documentation approach is that there is a solid quality foundation for everyone, and then the specifics are discussed in separate, industry-related documents, such as aerospace, automobile, and others.
