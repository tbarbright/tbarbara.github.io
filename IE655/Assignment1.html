Video 1 An Introduction to PLM and Digital Manufacturing Using DELMIA PLM Methods and Solutions Part 1:

What are the phases of digital manufacturing planning?

Conceptual planning includes assembly process planning where the entire process is planned using past knowledge and templates and 3D assembly process validation where the process is modeled and checked for problems.

Resource planning and resource programing determines the physical setup of the assembly line and what resources will be used where. This includes line balancing and workstation definition which includes flow simulation, automated station simulation, and manual station simulation.

Lastly execution puts the plan into action.

What is the benefit to using templates in the design process?

Templates describe how a certain part of the manufacturing process is usually done. They contain information on the typical process and parameters that must be considered. The benefit to this is that it allows the process planner to leverage past information in future plans and refine existing processes.

Why is digital manufacturing referees to as the glue between the product and the process?

Digital manufacturing relates a product to a process. Each step has a clear goal of creating some component of the product. The product or process can be refined based on this manufacturing plan.

What is the difference between a process planner and a 3D process planner and how do they work together?

A process planner describes a process in software and creates a detailed plan on implementation. A 3D process planner takes this process plan and checks it for accuracy and other issues. The 3D planner then builds a model of this process in software.

Why is line balancing and workstation definition important?

Line balancing and workstation definition is important because it can help identify bottle necks in the process and help stimulate the flow of the product from one station to another. Placing closely related stations near one another can aid in the smooth transition of a product from one area to another. Having too many stations near each other can create space issues. Line balancing can also help ensure that one area of the factory isn’t overwhelmed while other areas go unused.

Video 5 Digital Product Design: Design for Manufacturing (DFM) and Assembly (DFA) Principals and Practical Product Design Examples

Why is it advantageous to use digital design to produce parts with a single piece?

Producing parts in one piece reduces complexity by eliminating assembly steps. Single pieces cut with CNC machines can also have much tighter tolerances and be built stronger. This also makes maintenance easier because there are fewer parts to keep in stock.

Why is it important for designers to be experts in the assembly process?

Advanced machines such a mill turn machines can produce very complex designs as a single part. It is important for a designer to be familiar with the sorts of processes available to manufacturing. The designer can write the exact steps the machine needs to perform to complete the design.

What are the benefits to complex machines such as mill turn machines?

Mill turn machines can create complex parts by performing multiple operations on a single part. In the past, such complex shapes would require multiple machines to produce which increases complexity. Reducing the number of machines one needs to purchase can also keep costs down.

What steps can be taken to reduce the number of parts needed for a design?

We can eliminate parts or integrate the functions of that part into another part. We should also consider if we really need the part in the first place.

What is Z-Stacking and how can it help reduce complexity?

Z-Stacking is designing a product to be assembled in a single orientation. The less we need to rotate and move a product during assembly the simpler or assembly line can be. Each rotation or turn adds another step and potentially another machine to the assembly process.

Video 6 CNC Machine and Robot Integrated Cells and Flexible Agile Reconfigurable Manufacturing Systems

What is a CNC machine?

CNC stands for Computer Numerical Control. It is a manufacturing process that uses computers to control machines such as lathes, mills, routers, and grinders. CNC machines are used to automate the production of complex parts.

What are axises of control and why are they important?

An axis of control refers to a direction in which a machine is able to move. Many machines feature only two axises X and Y which allows for horizontal and vertical movement. More complex machines also employ a Z axis for movement up and down. These axises allow more complex and exotic parts to be produced.

What are chips or swarf and why must they be dealt with?

Chips are small pieces of material that are the remains of material removed from a metal blank during the milling process. If left in the machine they can get caught in the cutting tools and damage them or the product being produced. This leads to wasted time and materials.

How does a large tool magazine help the CNC machine?

A tool magazine can be used to rapidly and automatically change the tool that a particular machine is making. This allows for greater flexibility in designs. A single machine is able to perform more steps in rapid succession using this method. This also allows the use of “sister tools” or multiple tools of the same type that can be used if we are wearing out tools too fast.

What is a pallet and why are they used?

A pallet is a standardized holding system for CNC machines that can be used to transport products between machines. Pallet changers can be manual or automatically operated. This allows products to be easily moved from one machine to another with minimal intervention or to allow products to cool between machining.

Toyota made the seat more comfortable that its competitors designs. They also increased the size of the footwell to accommodate work-boots and the size of the handles to accommodate gloves. Toyota gathered data on forklift accidents and discovered that tip over accidents were the most common deadly accident. Kaizen is a Japanese word meaning continuous change for the better. One example of the Kaizen process is the design of the lifting fork itself. Video 9 Product Distribution & Supply Chain Management: Industrial RFID Smart Label Printing and Marking Engineering Design Challenges and Solutions

What advantages do RFID tags have to traditional barcodes?

RFID tags can be more easily read and tracked as they pass through locations. The scanner does not need to come into physical contact with them. Some large retailers require RFID tags as they have too much product to scan individually.

How are the RFID tags encoded?

The RFID tags come in a roll all with the same serial number. The printing device encodes the tag with a unique number in hexadecimal format. This allows a lot of data to be stored on the tag.

What may happen if a bad RFID tag is placed on a product label?

A bad RFID tag could result in customer products getting lost. If the machine is unable to read the tag product can be sent to the wrong location or not logged as it leaves the facility. The customer can get more or less product than they paid for. This would be unacceptable.

What steps does Weber take to ensure quality in its RFID tags?

Weber inspects each RFID tag it receives and removes the bad ones before they are shipped to customers. The machine which installs the tags then inspects them again before use and rejects bad tags by sticking them to a plate to be inspected later.

How does Weber fulfill the needs of customers who are not ready to use RFID?

Weber’s labeling machines are all RFID ready. A customer may purchase a regular label printer which can have RFID added it it later. This allows multiple types of customers to be served by a single machine.

Questions and Answers:

What is CE and PLM?

CE stands for concurrent engineering. It is a work methodology which focuses on the parallelization of tasks. The idea behind concurrent engineering is that working in parallel provides for greater understanding of the customer needs by all involved teams that must work closer together and also allows the final product to be delivered sooner. From a quality perspective, a concurrent design allows business to more quickly adapt to change and fix problems with a product. Concurrent engineering allows teams to identify problems sooner before they become major expensive issues requiring a redesign.

PLM or Product life-cycle management is the process of managing a product from its conception to design, implementation, usage, and eventual disposal. PLM defines a process for an enterprise to follow when developing a product and allows for the easy flow of information. PLM includes elements of Computer Aided Design (CAD), Computer Aided Manufacturing (CAM), Computer Aided Engineering (CAE), and Product Data Management (PDM.)

What is the difference between Concurrent Engineering (CE) and Product Life-Cycle Management (PLM)?

Concurrent Engineering is a process which is concerned with performing tasks in parallel to complete a project faster and reducing waste by increasing collaboration. Product Life-cycle Management tracks the specific stages of a product’s life and establishes methods for efficiently dealing with each step. PLM is more focused on specific systems.

What is green sustainable PLM?

Green sustainable PLM takes into account a product’s impact on the environment and aims to reduce this impact. For example a company may strive to reduce greenhouse gas emissions from their factories by using green energy. As PLM also deals with the death of products green sustainable PLM approach must consider what impact a product will have when thrown away. For example, a manufacturer may opt to use a more expensive but more recyclable material when building a product in order to reduce waste.

What is digital design & manufacture and why is this such a huge opportunity?

Digital design and manufacturing involves the usage of computer simulation and other software to aid in the design and manufacturing process for a product. This is a huge opportunity because it allows companies to design products virtually before committing any more money or resources to a physical product. This can help reduce waste because problems with designs can be identified before material is wasted and designs can be refined more quickly. For example, an auto manufacturer will build a virtual model of a car in order to judge aerodynamics before conducting an actual wind tunnel test.

How is digital design integrated with digital manufacturing in PLM?

Products should be designed for manufacturing (DFM). As a general rule, products should be designed to minimize the number of stages involved in the manufacturing process. When a product is designed digitally it can be then loaded into another program which can simulate a manufacturing setup. The digital design and digital manufacturing system can be fine tuned together before being put into production.

What is frontloading, and why is it critical to understand this process?

Frontloading is the act of building a substantial knowledge base using a cross functional team at the beginning of a design process. It is critical to have a well defined product and discuss potential issues as early as possible because changes become exponentially more expensive to make the further along in the process a project gets. If these issues are discovered early in the planning or digital design process they are much easier to fix than later on the factory floor.

What is TQM in the product, process, service system design context?

TQM or total quality management is a business approach focused on high quality. For products, this means a product which reaches the end user with no defects. List, and explain the various dimensions of design quality, with examples.

Brand name quality refers to what customers expect from your brand. What is your companies reputation? For example, a high end brand like Rolls Royce is synonymous with luxury and quality.

Features refers to the features of your product and how those features compare the competing products. For example, cell phone manufactures will often tout the specs of their new integrated cameras while various blogs will compare these cameras to other phones.

Performance refers to a product meeting or exceeding expectations. For example buyers of a high end sports car like Lamborghini or Ferrari expect a high performance car in regards to speed and handling.

Reliability refers to how often a product fails to perform. For example many cheap power tools from China are known to have issues with longevity which could negate the savings from purchasing the cheaper tool.

Serviceability refers to how easy a product is to service. For example many modern ultrabook style laptops are not easily disassembled and serviced by end users. Aesthetics refers to how good a product looks. Apple for example strives to make all of its products very minimalist looking and attempts to create a uniform product line that conveys a single brand image.

Excitement quality refers to how exciting a product is. For example a Ford Mustang may be perceived as a higher quality car than a Ford Focus because the Mustang just looks and feels more exciting.

Safety quality refers to how safe the product is. For example a modern car with various safety features such as seatbelts and airbags is much safer than an older car and therefore may be seen as higher quality.

Conformance to eco standards refers to how environmentally friendly a product is. For example a product can be made or recyclable materials to help reduce its carbon footprint.

Chapter 3:

What is MUDA and why should it be avoided?

MUDA is a Japanese word meaning waste. To the Japanese this includes any product functions which add no value, additional parts which are unnecessary, parts which are too complicated to produce or feature unnecessarily tight tolerances, parts which cost too much or are wasteful, and parts which are derived from toxic material. In order to avoid waste and improve quality these should be kept to a minimum.

Why should the number of parts in a design be minimized?

Products with fewer parts will cost less to produce. Manufacturers will also be able to reduce space used for parts storage. Simpler products are also easier to produce and easier to repair and can therefore be made higher quality.

What are ergonomics and why are they important?

Ergonomics is the science of designing items to fit the worker. Ergonomics can help an end user better enjoy a product and make it safer for use. Ergonomics can help prevent repetitive strain injuries which can lead to long term issues.

What are the hallmarks of a good industrial design?

A good industrial design optimizes the balance between function, value, and appearance. A good design should be sustainable and ergonomic and pleasing to use. The needs of users need to be taken into consideration.

What are the areas of focus for usability?

Usability is about making a product more useful. A useful product is more efficient, in that it makes a previously tedious task easier. A useful product is easy to learn and does not make the process it helps more complicated to learn. A useful product is satisfying to use than the old methods.
