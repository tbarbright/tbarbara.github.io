Virtual Company Name: Titan International Manufacturing

Titan International Manufacturing's goal is to create environmentally friendly, smart, and sustainable washing machines and driers for home use. Every day millions of Americans wash their laundry in so-called "dumb washing machines" that are old, outdated, energy inefficient, and lack the capabilities to filter harmful laundry byproducts like microplastics from entering the environment. Our smart washer will feature an intuitive interface and internet capabilities all with the goal of reducing waste.

FEMA/DRFA:

We performed an analysis of the filter install process for the Smart Wash Washing Machine. Based on this analysis we found that our greatest risk comes with our machine filter tests with an accumulated RPN of 4435.20. Our pressure test system needs to be more reliable to reduce the occurrence of false positives and false negatives. A false negative is more damaging as it could lead to a customer receiving a defective machine. The value here is particularly concerning as it reflects a high rate of failure of our pressure tests and hose fittings.

The high failure rate of pressure tests may be related to the high failure rate of the hose connection process with a RPN of 336.96. In turn we also noted that the filters had a high chance of becoming damaged during loading on the conveyor belt with an RPM of 280.28. If we can remove human error from this process it should help us down the line with our failed filter installations.

Based on this analysis we propose the following corrective actions:

Design a new process for loading filters onto the conveyor belt.

Re-evaluate filter inspection criteria.

Examine and adjust hose connection machine.

Adjust pressure test criteria.

Ranky-Weibull Calculator:

The Ranky-Weibull calculator can characterize failure distributions of our filter component. It has two basic parameters shape (β) and scale (α).

The average failure time for 20 samples was 1.84162 sec.

α which indicates the spread is 1.53.

β which indicates whether the failure rate is increasing, constant, or decreasing is 2.35.

β > 1 increasing failure rate.

β = 1 constant failure rate.

β > 1 increasing failure rate.

Since β is 2.35, the samples have an increasing failure rate.

This high rate of failure agrees with the results of the DRFA sheet. We need to refine our filter process.
